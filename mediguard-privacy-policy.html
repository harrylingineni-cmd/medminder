Privacy Policy for MediGuard

Last updated: 18 July 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how the MediGuard mobile application ("MediGuard", "the app", "we", "us") handles your information. MediGuard is a medication reminder app with an optional brand-name and active-ingredient lookup. Please read this policy carefully. By using MediGuard, you agree to the practices described here.

In short: Your saved medication schedule stays on your device. MediGuard has no account system or server and does not use advertising or tracking. If you choose United States in Find Generic, the medicine name you search for is sent directly to the U.S. National Library of Medicine's RxNav service.

Information the App Stores

To do its job, MediGuard lets you enter and stores the following information locally on your device:

  • The names of your medications
  • Their dosages
  • The times you want to be reminded
  • Your reminder settings (such as how long reminders should repeat)
  • Records of whether you have marked a dose as taken or snoozed

This information is saved only in the app's local storage on your phone. Your saved schedule, dosage, reminder times, and taken/snoozed records are not included in Find Generic requests.

Find Generic and the RxNav Service

The India Find Generic list works offline. If you select United States and tap Search, MediGuard sends the medicine name typed into the search box directly to the National Library of Medicine's RxNav service over an encrypted HTTPS connection. RxNav returns medicine and active-ingredient information to the app.

  • MediGuard does not send your saved medication schedule, dosage, reminder history, name, email address, or location with the search.
  • MediGuard does not receive or store a copy of your search on a MediGuard server because MediGuard has no server.
  • The National Library of Medicine may automatically record information about visits and searches as described in its Web Policies.

MediGuard uses publicly available NLM data. NLM does not endorse or recommend MediGuard and is not responsible for the app.

Information We Do NOT Collect

MediGuard does not:

  • Require you to create an account or log in
  • Collect your name, email address, phone number, or location
  • Upload your saved medication schedule or reminder history to a MediGuard server
  • Keep a MediGuard copy of your Find Generic searches
  • Use analytics, advertising, or tracking tools
  • Sell your information

Permissions the App Uses

MediGuard requests certain phone permissions only so that reminders work correctly:

  • Notifications — to show you reminder alerts at your chosen times.
  • Alarms & reminders (exact alarms) — so reminders appear at the precise scheduled time.
  • Internet access — only for United States Find Generic searches and opening web links such as this privacy policy.

Notification and alarm permissions are used only for on-device reminders. Internet access does not upload your saved medication schedule.

How Your Information Is Stored and Deleted

All saved medication, reminder, and taken/snoozed information stays on your device. MediGuard does not store a history of Find Generic searches. Because MediGuard stores no user data on its own server, you remain in control of the app's local data:

  • You can delete any medication from within the app at any time.
  • Uninstalling the app removes all of the information it has stored on your device.

Children's Privacy

MediGuard is intended for use by adults, including older adults and caregivers. It is not directed at children under the age of 13, and we do not knowingly collect information from children.

Medical Disclaimer

MediGuard is a reminder and informational lookup tool only. It is not a medical device, and it does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, treatment, or an automatic recommendation to substitute one product for another. It does not guarantee that a medication has been taken, and it is not a substitute for professional medical care or the advice of a doctor or pharmacist. Always follow the instructions of your healthcare provider and check the exact medication, strength, dosage form, and label before taking or switching any medication. In an emergency, contact your local emergency services.
